# Fingerprinting & Data Handling Disclosure

**Last Updated:** January 15, 2026

## Purpose of This Disclosure

This disclosure explains how Hephzibah King's Mother handles fingerprint and biometric data for Level 2 background screening services in compliance with federal and state regulations.

## Authorization and Compliance

Hephzibah King's Mother is an approved LiveScan provider authorized by:
- **Florida Department of Law Enforcement (FDLE)**
- **Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)**

We comply with all applicable regulations governing fingerprint-based background screening, including:
- Florida Statutes Chapter 435 (Employment Screening)
- FBI Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) Security Policy
- Florida Administrative Code requirements for LiveScan providers

## What is Level 2 Background Screening?

A Level 2 background screening is a comprehensive fingerprint-based check that searches:
- State criminal history records (via FDLE)
- National criminal history records (via FBI)
- Sex offender and sexual predator registries
- Additional databases as required by law

This screening is required for positions of trust involving:
- Working with children
- Working with vulnerable adults (elderly, disabled, or dependent persons)
- Healthcare positions
- Childcare and education positions
- Other positions designated by law

## How Fingerprint Data is Collected

### In-Person Collection Only
**Important:** No biometric or fingerprint data is collected through our website or any online form. All fingerprint data is collected **in person only** using authorized LiveScan equipment.

### Collection Process
1. **Scheduling:** You schedule an appointment via our website or phone
2. **Identification:** You present valid government-issued photo identification at your appointment
3. **LiveScan Capture:** We capture your fingerprints using FBI-certified LiveScan equipment
4. **Transmission:** Fingerprint data is transmitted directly to FDLE/FBI databases
5. **Processing:** FDLE/FBI process the background check and return results to the requesting agency

## How We Handle Your Data

### Data We Collect
During your fingerprinting appointment, we collect:
- Basic identifying information (name, date of birth, address)
- Fingerprint biometric data
- Reason for screening (employment, licensing, etc.)
- Requesting agency information

### Data Transmission
- Fingerprint data is encrypted and transmitted directly to FDLE/FBI systems
- Transmission occurs in real-time using secure, authorized channels
- We use FBI-certified LiveScan equipment and software

### Data Storage
**Critical Protection:** We do **not** store fingerprint biometric data on our local systems. 

- Fingerprints are captured and immediately transmitted
- We retain only the transaction record and demographic information as required for audit purposes
- No biometric templates or fingerprint images are kept on site
- Transaction records are maintained in accordance with FDLE requirements

### Data Security
We maintain physical, technical, and administrative safeguards:
- Secure facility access controls
- Encrypted data transmission
- CJIS Security Policy compliance
- Regular security training for staff
- Background-screened personnel only

## Use and Disclosure of Results

Background screening results are:
- Transmitted directly to the requesting agency (employer, licensing board, etc.)
- Governed by applicable employment screening laws
- Subject to federal and state confidentiality requirements
- Not disclosed to unauthorized parties

We do not:
- Make hiring or licensing decisions (these are made by requesting agencies)
- Guarantee specific outcomes or timeframes (processing is performed by FDLE/FBI)
- Provide results directly to the individual being screened (results go to requesting agency)

## Retention and Disposal

- Transaction records are retained per FDLE requirements (typically 3-5 years)
- No biometric data is retained
- Records are securely disposed of in compliance with CJIS requirements when retention period expires

## Processing Timeframes

Processing times vary and are controlled by FDLE and FBI, typically:
- Florida-only checks: 24-72 hours
- National FBI checks: 3-5 business days (may be longer)
- Results are sent directly to the requesting agency

We cannot expedite or guarantee specific processing times, as these are determined by state and federal agencies.
